Steelmaking Expanded adds a full industrial pipeline for iron/steel production that works in two stages. In Stage I you will build a Blast Furnace and its support machinery to smelt iron in bulk. Stage II bolts a Bessemer Converter onto that line to refine the molten iron into steel. Build the iron stage first and get it running reliably — the steel stage is expensive and feeds directly off it.
Blast furnace requires tier3 refractory bricks, which means you need to reach the steel age before starting working on this! Be aware that all of this works only when powered with Steam so try building a steam power setup before attempting this.

The full production chain is described in four companion articles:
- The Blast Furnace — the heart of the operation and where you should start Stage I.
- Hot Blast & Gas Handling — describes cowper stoves, the smoke stack and interaction with pipe network. You cannot fire furnace and achieve iron melting point without these!
- Molten Metal & Casting — work with canals, taps and molds to produce desired items. Route overflow molten metal or waste slag into special barrels.
- The Bessemer Converter — Stage II begins here, allowing you to use heavy machinery to blow iron into steel. Utilizes all previous mechanics so be ready!

The pipes, boilers and steam engines that power it all come from the companion mod — see Steam Power. These are utilized to produce mechanical power and to pressurize air to required pressure so it could work as blast.

Here is the quick brief of the complete production run for iron:
1. Stock the Reinforced Hopper of the Blast Furnace with crushed iron ore, crushed coke and lime, and enable dropping of the Bell Hopper.
2. Let the shaft of the furnace fill with blast mix piles, after which ignite it with a torch, and close the door.
3. Push Air through a Heated up Cowper Stove into the Tuyeres as hot blast (a powered Air Blower on a Steam Engine provides the required air and pressure). Vent excess gases through the Smoke Stack.
4. Watch the temperature climb past ~1482 °C into the Melting phase while alternating your Two Cowper Stoves so the blast never goes cold.
5. When iron and slag have pooled, open the Molten Metal Taps to drain them down the canals into Molds or Barrels. Be ware it can solidify!
6. Keep the hopper supplied, the exhaust flowing, boilers boiling and repeat.

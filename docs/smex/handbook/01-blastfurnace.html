The Blast Furnace
The blast furnace is the heart of the operation: a tall multiblock built mostly from refractory bricks, with an iron door, two tuyeres (air intakes) low on the shaft and two gas outlets high up. Place the door block and shift + rightmouse it to see the build outline, then fill in every highlighted block until the structure reports complete.

Blocks required:
- 98 x Refractory Bricks (Tier 3)
- 1 x Blast Furnace Door (the control block, placed first)
- 2 x Tuyere
- 2 x Molten Metal Tap
- 2 x Gas Outlet (Refractory brick Tier3)
- 1 x Reinforced Hopper
- 1 x Bell Hopper
The hollow shaft inside is left empty — the hoppers fill it with blast mix.

Feeding it. The furnace does not eat ore directly! It burns Blast Mix, a packed pile of ore and fuel. You cannot craft blast mix by hand — it is produced by the pair of hoppers stacked above the shaft. The Reinforced Hopper is the magazine: open it (rightmouse) and load three things — crushed iron ore, crushed coke, and lime (flux). Crush iron ore and coke in a pulverizer, coke is baked from coal in a coke oven, and even scrap iron bits can be crushed back into crushed iron for resmelting. The hopper combines 12 crushed iron + 3 crushed coke + 1 lime into 16 blast mix and stockpiles it internally (total 60 units of iron).

Below it sits the Bell Hopper, which drips the stockpiled blast mix down into the shaft. Toggle dropping with ctrl + rightmouse on either or the hoppers. The dropping stops on its own once the furnace is full.

Fuel pile broken? Blast mix behaves just like a coal pile: rightmouse the ground to lay loose mix back down, stack more onto it, and feed it back to the furnace through hoppers. It even burns as a normal fuel in a pinch, but don't expect much from it.

Firing it. Let the bell hopper fill the furnace, after which ignite the blast mix inside, and close the door. Once every pile in the hearth is burning, the door is shut and the exhaust outlets are not choked, the furnace begins firing and climbs toward ~1400 °C on its own, but you need to hold it above iron's melting point (~1482 °C, see the Hot Blast article) for a few minutes and it enters the Melting phase, converting blast mix into a reservoir of molten iron and molten slag.

The fire is fragile! Opening the door, running out of blast mix, a choked or backfed exhaust, or letting the molten reservoir overflow will all snuff it out — and an extinguished melt solidifies in the hearth as iron blocks you must mine out. Keep the exhaust flowing and the door shut while it works the iron.

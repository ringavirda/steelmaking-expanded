What is hot blast? Preheated air under pressure that allows chemical processes and smelting occur inside the furnace. For the purposes of this production, blast is air under pressure higher than 2.5 atm and temperature higher than 800 °C.

Why hot blast? A raw blast furnace tops out around 1400 °C — not enough to melt iron. To push past that you reclaim the heat it throws away through exhaust.

The gas network. Everything breathes through pipes (from Pipes and Power Expanded). In this situation we will work with gas in three kinds: ordinary Air, hot Exhaust spilling from the furnace, and forced Blast (pressurized air). You need to setup a few specific machines and structures for this to work. For the air/exhaust you will need:
- The Air Blower — a steam-engine sub-machine that pressurizes Air into Blast. This is what forces gas through the cowper stoves and into the furnace through tuyeres.
- Valves open and close a line by hand — essential for swapping the cowper stoves around and controlling the flow inside pipe networks.
- Pressure valves open automatically above their gate pressure, allowing overflow to another side (input side is marked with copper textured rim).
- Outlets connect the network to a structure's face and are a part of their multiblock footprint.
Each network tracks its gas type, volume and temperature; an open-ended pipe leaks. Think carefully how many machines you need so you won't under or over supply!

The Cowper Stove — a heat regenerator. Pipe the furnace's Exhaust into a Cowper Stove and it soaks that heat into its heatsink at the core (\"charging\"); burning a coal pile inside charges it faster, with anthracite fastest. Beware: burning low grades of coal makes the stove itself produce exhaust from its main output!
Once charged, route plain Blast through the same stove and it pours out scorching hot. Feed that into the furnace tuyeres and the internal temperature will start rising again toward ~1700 °C.

Blocks required (per stove — you want two):
- 48 x Refractory Bricks (any tier)
- 1 x Cowper Stove Intake (the control block)
- 4 x Heat Sink
- 2 x Gas Outlet (any refractory)
- 1 x Gas Passthrough (any refractory)
- 1 x Iron Hatch Door
One slot below the intake is left open for the coal pile that speeds up the heat exchange.

A single stove only does one job at a time, and a charged core runs down as you draw blast from it. Run two stoves as a pair: while one charges on exhaust, the other discharges hot blast for the furnace. When the discharging one cools down below 800 °C, swap their roles with valves. Keep exhaust and air on separate paths — if they mix inside a stove it cannot heat up.

The Smoke Stack. A blast furnace produces more exhaust than the stoves can swallow. The Smoke Stack vents the surplus to the sky — a backed-up exhaust line chokes and extinguishes the furnace, so treat it as the safety valve of the whole system.
Blocks required:
- 1 x Smoke Stack Intake (the control block)
- 24 x Refractory Bricks (any tier)
- 36 x Any Bricks

Don't forget about the Stoves! All of this doesn't operate on it's own and you need to keep an eye on the stoves' temperature. Otherwise your furnace may stop smelting iron or even extinguish!

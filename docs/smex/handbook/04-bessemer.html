What is the Bessemer Process? It is a way you can turn raw iron into steel. The idea is that iron contains to much pollution in form of excess carbon, sulfur and other elements. You need to literally boil the iron by blowing blast through it, firing out these pollutants and purifying iron to the state of mild steel.

The Bessemer Converter is the machine that takes molten iron and blows it into steel. It is the most demanding machine in the mod — it draws both mechanical power and a blast supply, and only works as the final stage of an already-running molten line. Do not attempt it until your iron tier is established!

Building it. The converter is a 3x3x3 vessel raised through right-click construction. Place its control block first, then rightmouse it with 4 rusty gears and 12 iron or steel rods in your hotbar to start the vessel, and build up the construction stages until it reports complete. Around the vessel sit its service ports: a gas intake, a mechanical transmission, an input tap, and an output canal start block.

Blocks required:
- 1 x Bessemer Control (the control block, placed first)
- 1 x Bessemer Converter — raised in place from 4 rusty gears + 12 iron/steel rods
- 1 x Bessemer Transmission
- 1 x Bessemer Gas Intake
- 1 x Molten Canal (Start)
- 2 x Molten Canal (Straight)
- 1 x Molten Canal (Tap)

Construction cost for the converter vessel itself. All stages together consume:
- 40 x Iron/Steel Plate
- 42 x Iron/Steel Nails & Strips
- 18 x Iron/Steel Rod
- 84 x Refractory Brick (tier 3)
- 84 x Fire Clay
- 3 x Gas Piping (Straight)

Powering it. Two services from the systems you already built:
- Mechanical power. Connect an axle to the transmission so the vessel can tilt and blow. Recommended to use steam engine to power this due to torque required.
- Blast. Pipe pressured air from a blower into the gas intake of the converter. The intake must face the same way as the control block or it won't couple.
Then run a molten canal from a furnace tap into the converter's input tap, and a second canal below will carry its output toward your molds.

Operating it.
1. Set the converter to Filling and pour molten iron in from the canal until charged to your desired amount (max amount not required for operation).
2. Switch to Normal. With power and blast flowing, it refines the charge at ~1800 °C for about five minutes into steel.
3. Set it to Pouring to drain the finished steel into the output canal — from there it travels the molten network exactly like iron.

The same temperature rule applies inside the vessel! If the charge cools and solidifies before you pour it, break the converter with a steel pickaxe to recover the metal, then rebuild. So make sure you can drain the vessel before it solidifies.

Notes. It is required to have minimum two converters to consume all of the output iron of a single blast furnace. You may start with one but if you truly want to achieve continuous steel production operate two converters. All of the machinery required for production take a lot of space, both horizontal and vertical. Also, make sure that you can access easily all of the valves and controls, so you won't waste time trying to reach something while your molten metal solidifies or furnace cools. This may be the time you finally built that factory you always wanted to have!

Molten Canals. Liquid metal is plumbed, not carried. Lay a Molten Canal (Start) as the anchor of a network, then run straights, bends and junctions from it — they are crafted from cobblestone and fire clay. Drain the furnace with its Molten Metal Taps — the lower tap pours iron, the upper tap pours slag. There should be a start of a canal below it, otherwise it won't allow you to toggle it with rightmouse. A Canal Tap at the far end pours the metal into barrel, if you have excess material and need to drain the canals. (Note that molten iron flow is simulated so not everything may be drained.)

Sealing — a manual valve. rightmouse an empty straight canal with 4 fire clay and it caps over, severing the network at that block. Only a drained canal can be sealed. Reopen it with a chisel (recovers 2 fire clay). This can be used to manually select which canal path the metal will take, if you have multiple possible ways for it to go.

Mind the temperature. Metal in the canals slowly cools! If it drops too far it solidifies in place. A hardened canal stops flowing and must be chipped clear. Use Chisel + Hammer once it cools enough to recover most of the metal as bits, and those bits can be crushed back into crushed iron for the furnace. Keep runs short, keep them fed, and empty them before they or the furnace go cold.

Casting. A canal tap pours into two destinations:
- A Mold Pedestal casts small tool molds: shift + rightmouse places or removes the mold, ctrl + rightmouse toggles pouring. This mod adds larger casting molds — plate mold, quad rod mold and double ingot mold that you clay-form and fire like any tool mold. The big anvil and helve hammer molds go under the canal tap itself instead.
- A Molten Barrel — bulk storage for liquid metal. Use it to buffer excess iron, or to hold slag from the top tap. When contents solidify, you can also chisel it out like solidified canal, simply place the barrel on the ground first.

Handling hot molds. A freshly filled mold is dangerously hot! Carrying it bare-handed burns you (wear heavy leather or blacksmith's gloves). While the metal is still liquid, the mold can only ride in your empty active hand — stashing it in a bag, or racking it spills the metal instantly. Set it down and let it harden, then collect the casting with a plain rightmouse.

Slag is not waste. Solidified slag grinds into powdered slag: mixed with slaked lime in a barrel it yields mortar, and spread on farmland it acts as a phosphate fertilizer — furnace byproduct into building stock and crop yield.

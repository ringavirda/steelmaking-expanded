An industrial iron- and steel-making chain for Vintage Story: a multiblock blast furnace, gas and molten-metal pipe networks, a regenerative hot-blast loop, and a Bessemer converter that turns molten iron into steel.

Steelmaking Expanded replaces "stand at a bloomery" with a real production line. You smelt iron in bulk in a blast furnace (Tier I), then optionally refine it into steel in a Bessemer converter (Tier II). Gas pipes move air, blast and exhaust between the machines; molten canals plumb the liquid metal to molds and storage.

Progression gate: most of these structures are built from tier-3 refractory bricks, so you need to be in the steel age already before you can build the line. Get the iron tier running reliably before you attempt the steel tier — the converter is expensive and feeds directly off your iron output.

Tier I — Iron Production

Blast Furnace

[ Screenshot: completed furnace with door, tuyeres and gas outlets labelled ]

A tall multiblock built mostly from refractory bricks, with an iron door, two tuyeres (air intakes) low on the shaft and two gas outlets high up.

Build: place the door block, then Shift + Right-click it to show the build outline. Fill in every highlighted block until it reports complete.

Operate:

  • It doesn't eat ore directly — it burns blast mix (ore + fuel + flux), produced for you by the hoppers (below).
  • Let the shaft fill, ignite the blast mix, and close the door.
  • It climbs to ~1400 °C on its own; feed it hot blast from a cowper stove to push past iron's melting point (~1482 °C) into the Melting phase, which converts blast mix into a reservoir of molten iron and molten slag.
  • The fire is fragile: opening the door, running out of blast mix, a choked/backfed exhaust, or an overflowing reservoir will all snuff it out.

Hoppers & Coke Oven (furnace feed)

[ Screenshot: reinforced hopper + bell hopper stacked above the shaft ]

  • Coke Oven — bakes coal into coke.
  • Reinforced Hopper — the magazine. Load crushed iron ore + crushed coke + lime (flux); it combines 12 + 3 + 1 into 16 blast mix at a time and stockpiles it internally.
  • Bell Hopper — sits below and drips blast mix into the shaft. Toggle dropping with Ctrl + Right-click on the reinforced hopper; it auto-stops when the furnace is full.

So loading the furnace is really just keeping the reinforced hopper supplied.

Cowper Stove — hot blast

[ Screenshot: a pair of cowper stoves with gas piping ]

A raw furnace tops out around 1400 °C. The cowper stove is a regenerator that reclaims the furnace's wasted heat:

  • Pipe the furnace's Exhaust into it to "charge" its brick core (burning a coal pile beneath charges it faster; anthracite is fastest).
  • Then route plain Blast (pressurised air) through the charged stove — it comes out scorching hot. Feed that into the tuyeres and the furnace jumps toward ~1700 °C.

Intended setup: two stoves as a pair. While one charges on exhaust, the other discharges hot blast; when the discharging one cools, swap their roles with valves. Keep exhaust and blast on separate paths — if they mix inside a stove it can't heat. (Burning low-grade coal produces exhaust out the stove's main output — mind your piping.)

Smoke Stack

[ Screenshot: smoke stack venting ]

A furnace makes more exhaust than the stoves can swallow. The smoke stack vents the surplus to the sky so the exhaust line doesn't back up — a backed-up exhaust chokes and extinguishes the furnace. Treat it as a safety valve for the whole system.

Tier II — Steel Production

Bessemer Converter

[ Screenshot: converter mid-construction and complete ]

Takes molten iron and blows it into steel. The most demanding machine in the mod — it costs a lot of materials, needs both mechanical power and a blast supply, and only works as the final stage of an already-running molten line.

Build: a 3×3×3 vessel raised through right-click construction. Place the control block, Right-click it with 4 rusty gears + 12 iron/steel rods to start the vessel, then build up the stages until complete. Around it sit a gas intake, a transmission, an input tap, and an output.

Wire it in:

  • Mechanical power — connect an axle to the transmission so the vessel can tilt and blow.
  • Blast — pipe a blast supply (from a blower) into the gas intake. The intake must face the same way as the control block or it won't couple.
  • Run a molten canal from a furnace tap into the input tap, and a second canal off the output toward your molds.

Operate:

  1. Set to Filling and pour molten iron into the input tap until charged.
  2. Switch to Normal — with power and blast flowing it blows air through the charge at ~1600 °C for about five minutes, refining it to steel.
  3. Set to Pouring to drain the finished steel into the output canal.

If the charge cools and solidifies before you pour it, break the converter with a steel pickaxe to recover the metal, then rebuild.

The Gas Network

[ Screenshot: gas pipe run with intake, blower and valves ]

Everything breathes through gas pipes. Gas comes in three kinds: Air, hot Exhaust, and pressurised Blast. Straight pipes, bends, T- and X-junctions carry it, plus a few special blocks:

  • Intakes — draw fresh Air from the open world.
  • Blowers — axle-powered; pressurise Air into Blast (this is what drives the cowper stove and the converter).
  • Valves — open/close a line by hand (essential for swapping the two cowper stoves).
  • Pressure valves — open automatically once enough pressure builds behind them.
  • Outlets — connect the network to a structure's face (tuyere, stove, converter intake).

Each network tracks its gas type, volume and temperature. An open-ended pipe leaks, so cap your runs into a structure.

The Molten Network

[ Screenshot: canal run from a furnace tap into a mold pedestal ]

Liquid metal is plumbed, not carried.

  • Lay a Molten Canal (Start) as the anchor of a network, then run straights, bends and junctions from it.
  • Drain the furnace with its Molten Metal Taps — the lower tap pours iron, the upper pours slag — each into the start of a canal below it (Right-click to toggle).
  • A Canal Tap at the far end pours metal back out, into one of two destinations:
    • Mold Pedestal — place a fired mold on it and it fills from the network. This mod adds larger casting molds: plate, quad rod and double ingot (clay-formed and fired like any tool mold).
    • Molten Barrel — bulk storage; buffer excess iron, or hold slag drained off the top tap.

Mind the temperature. Metal in the canals cools, and if it drops too far it solidifies in place — a hardened canal stops flowing and must be broken to clear it (you recover most of the metal as bits). Keep runs short, keep them fed, and empty them before they go cold.

Sealing a straight canal (manual valve). Right-click an empty straight canal with 4 fire clay and it caps over on both ends, severing the network there so the two sides become independent runs — handy for isolating a section. A canal that still holds metal refuses to seal. Right-click a sealed canal with a chisel to break the seal and recover 2 fire clay.

A freshly filled mold is dangerously hot: holding one bare-handed burns you (wear heavy leather or blacksmith's gloves), and a still-molten mold will spill if you pick it up or rack it before it sets.

Byproducts & extra uses

[ Screenshot: slag → powdered slag → mortar / fertilizer ]

  • Slag tapped off the furnace can be ground into powdered slag, which has two uses:
    • mixed with slaked lime in a barrel → mortar (the pozzolanic powder reacts with the lime and stands in for the sand in an ordinary mortar mix);
    • spread on farmland → a phosphate fertilizer with a lasting soil boost.
  • Blast mix is never wasted. It behaves like a coal pile — if you end up holding loose blast mix (knocking a pile out of the shaft, breaking a hopper), just Right-click the ground to lay it back into a pile, or burn it as fuel.

Vanilla overrides

This mod swaps a few vanilla classes for drop-in replacements so the molten line behaves correctly. Nothing changes vanilla recipes or balance — these only add the plumbing the new machines need:

  • Tool mold — filled molds produced by the mold pedestal can now be picked up, placed, and racked while still carrying their metal (vanilla molds never carry contents in the item stack, so the metal would otherwise vanish). A still-molten mold spills if placed or racked before it sets.
  • Mold rack — spills a molten mold the instant it's racked (you must let it set first).
  • Coal pile — gains blast-mix behavior: a pile of blast mix burns down into solidified slag after a while, unless a blast furnace is actively consuming it.
  • Tool mold patch — adds the larger plate / double-ingot / quad-rod mold variants and a two-handed hold for molds.

Helpful notes

  • Build and debug the iron tier first; only add the converter once canals fill reliably.
  • Point at any machine to read its live status in the block-info overlay (charge, temperature, what it's waiting on).
  • Cap every gas run into a structure — open pipe ends leak.
  • Keep molten runs short and fed; cold metal solidifies and has to be broken out.
  • The in-game handbook ("Advanced Steelmaking") has the full step-by-step walkthrough.

FAQ

My blast furnace keeps going out.
Check the usual suspects: the door is open, you're out of blast mix, the exhaust line is choked/backed-up (add a smoke stack), or the molten reservoir overflowed. The exhaust must keep flowing and the door must stay shut while it works.

The furnace won't get hot enough to melt iron.
A raw furnace tops out around 1400 °C. You need hot blast — charge a cowper stove on exhaust, then blow pressurised air through it into the tuyeres. One stove runs down as you draw from it, so use two and alternate them with valves.

My gas network isn't doing anything / says it's leaking.
An open-ended pipe leaks. Make sure runs terminate in an outlet coupled to a structure, that a blower (on an axle) is pressurising your Blast line, and that exhaust and blast aren't sharing a stove.

The Bessemer converter won't start.
It needs all of: a completed vessel, mechanical power on the transmission, a Blast supply on the gas intake, and a charge of molten iron. The gas intake must face the same direction as the control block or it won't couple.

My metal solidified in the canal / converter.
It cooled too far before you poured it. Break the affected blocks to recover most of the metal as bits, then keep runs shorter and better fed. For the converter, break it with a steel pickaxe and rebuild.

How do I make a shut-off in a canal?
Right-click an empty straight canal with 4 fire clay to seal it into a separator. Right-click it with a chisel to reopen and get 2 fire clay back. Canals with metal still in them can't be sealed.

What do I do with all the slag?
Grind it into powdered slag and either make mortar (with slaked lime in a barrel) or use it as phosphate fertilizer on farmland.

I picked up some blast mix by accident — is it lost?
No. It works like a coal pile: lay it back down with Right-click, or burn it as fuel.

Does this change vanilla balance/recipes?
No. The only vanilla classes it replaces are plumbing for molds, mold racks and coal piles (see Vanilla overrides); it doesn't touch vanilla recipes or progression beyond requiring the steel age to build the machines.

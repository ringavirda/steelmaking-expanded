Engines take steam from a pipe network and drive one attached sub-machine. Place the engine first (it raises through right-click construction stages, like boilers), then place its sub-machine in the cell just under the pumping rod. Submachine snaps into the right facing on its own.

Watt Engine is an engine that operates on large amounts of low pressure steam. It is relatively cheap and can be used to stand up your first steam power setups. One Cornish boiler can safely power one of these engines, and it:
- Runs at 2-4 atm inlet steam; above 4 atm it wears down fast and can break!
- Draws 30 L/s of steam, delivers 0.3 power (enough to power 2 helve hammers without the speed dropping), and dribbles ~1 L/s of hot condensate out its drain. You can pipe it away to reuse the water or it spills on the floor.
- Construction stages consume: 4 x Metal Plate, 24 x Metal Rod, 12 x Metal Nails & Strips, all iron or steel and 36 fire bricks.

Cornish Engine is more advanced engine that works on higher pressured steam. It consumes less steam volume to produce the same power as Watt engine, and since it can regulate the amount of steam entering the cylinder - you can throttle this engine:
- Runs at 6-8 atm. Its steam control rod sets the working rate: Low / Normal / High that will draw 8 / 16 / 32 L/s of steam respectively and deliver 0.2 / 0.4 / 0.8 units of power. So overclocked Cornish engine can easily power 6 helve hammers, and throttling also affects the amount of water is pumped using the fluid pump.
- Construction stages consume the same as watt engine, the difference in cost is in the main block.

Sub-machines are the blocks that consume engine's power to do actual work (one per engine, placed in the engine's drive cell). The steam engine is only the power provider, so depending on different sub-machines it can do different work:
- MP Generator — turns engine's power into usable mechanical power. It is a constant-power source for the axles: within the engine's rated load it holds normal speed. If you pile on more machines and the whole network slows past roughly double the rated load it overstresses and stops. A Watt engine comfortably runs a couple of helve hammers, a Cornish engine on High can probably run a whole workshop.
- Fluid Pump — transfers water from its intake line into its output line, about ~5 L/s at full engine power. Its output is pressurised to ~75% of the engine's inlet steam pressure — enough to push water up into a boiler. Pumps double the amount of water if powered by overclocked Cornish engine.
- Air Blower (from Steelmaking Expanded) — pressurises air into Blast for the blast furnace and Bessemer converter. It also cannot pressurize the air more than ~75% of the engine's inlet steam pressure, so manage your steam levels with pressure valves to achieve required pressure. This sub-machine produces ~16 L/s of air if powered by normal running engine and double that when powered by pressured up Cornish.

Breakage and repair. An engine run above its pressure band for too long breaks: it's cylinder explodes and piston is damaged. Click on it with rightmouse while holding a wrench to see the repair bill, then with the materials in your hotbar to fix it. Mind the Cornish-boiler-into-Watt-engine trap: that boiler can push 5 atm, the Watt breaks at 4 — regulate with a pressure valve.

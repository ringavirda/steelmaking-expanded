Boilers allow to burn coal near a water vessel and push steam into a pipe network. Boiling 1 L of water yields 16 L of steam, at 160-220 °C. This steam builds up inside the boiler and it's connected steam network.

Construction. Place the boiler block, then rightmouse it repeatedly with the required materials in your hotbar to raise it through its construction stages. The boiler also needs a masonry firebox built around its base — ctrl + shift + rightmouse shows the outline, and the chat reports what is missing.

Cornish Boiler is a basic single flue boiler that can be used for supplying the machines with low pressure steam. It is recommended for you to start building these before you go for the next tier. Materials required for it's construction:
- 24 x Metal Plate (iron or steel)
- 18 x Metal Nails & Strips (iron or steel)
- 8 x Metal Rod (iron or steel)
- 48 x Fire Brick
Firebox masonry: 33 x Fire Brick Blocks, 1 x Coke Oven Door, 2 x Fire Brick Pipe Passthrough, 1 x Fire Brick Passthrough Bend (up), 1 x Fire Brick Pipe Outlet (up), one empty slot for the coal pile.
This boiler outputs up to 32 L/s of steam and chokes at 5 atm. Vessel holds 800 L while requiring 150 L of water to actually boil.

Lancashire Boiler is bigger and more advanced boiler that can produce higher pressure steam at bigger volumes. This one is required to operate the machinery that requires higher pressures, but be ready for the steel amounts needed to build one of these:
- 36 x Steel Plate
- 24 x Metal Nails & Strips (iron or steel)
- 12 x Metal Rod (iron or steel)
- 64 x Fire Brick
Firebox masonry: 39 x Fire Brick Blocks plus the same door, passthroughs and outlet as the Cornish.
This boiler can output up to 48 L/s of steam and can reach up to 12 atm of pressure. It's vessel holds 1200 L while requiring at least 200 L of water to begin boiling.

Operating a boiler. You need to kickstart the system manually with buckets, after which the running boiler can power the engine for the fluid pump so it will start refilling automatically. The key is to reach steam pressure required for the engine to start operating before the manually filled water runs out. If this operation goes smoothly, the pumping engine will be able to supply your boilers so they will run while the coal is burning.
1. Hold rightmouse on the lid to open it, pour in water (buckets, or pipe pressurised water into the intake from a fluid pump), and close the lid.
2. Lay a coal pile in the firebox slot behind the door and ignite it.
3. After about three minutes of heating the boiler starts boiling and pushes steam out its top outlet. This is what needs to go to your engines!
4. The firebox exhaust must breathe: vent its outlet through a chimney or pipe. If exhaust back-pressure rises above ~0.8 atm the fire goes out.

The boilers are dangerous! A boiler that keeps firing with nowhere to push steam and sits at its choke pressure can explode! An open lid vents steam harmlessly (≈100 L/s), this is your emergency release. An unpiped steam outlet just leaks, but it is not enough to prevent the boiler from overpressuring. Feeding pressurised water into a boiling vessel flashes extra steam and spikes the pressure! You need to throttle pumped feed water with a valve or gate it.
Warning! The Cornish boiler's 5 atm ceiling is above the Watt engine's 4 atm breaking point. Put a pressure valve between boiler and engine and create an outflow pipe to keep the pressure under control.

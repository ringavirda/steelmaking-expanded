The smaller blocks that can shape a pipe network:

Valve — a hand-operated shut-off in the line. rightmouse toggles it: open, the line flows through; closed, the network is severed at the valve and the two sides become independent. Sits flush against machine ports.

Pressure Valve — a directional overflow. It holds back its input side until the pressure rises above its gate pressure, then lets the excess through to its output side; wrench it to flip the direction, rightmouse / ctrl + rightmouse to raise or lower the gate. With nothing piped on the output it vents the excess like a leak. The essential regulator between a strong boiler and a weak engine. The input side is marked with copper textured ring.

Steam Condenser — used to convert unused steam back into water to safely recycle it. The condenser pulls up to 30 L/s of steam from its steam port and adds the condensed water (1 L per 16 L of steam) to its separate water line, which passes through it at up to 50 L/s. Use it to reclaim boiler feed water and to relieve steam pressure.

Fluid Intake — draws fresh water from open water into the network. It must sit directly on a full cube of water at least 3 blocks deep, and refuses to work within 6 blocks of another intake. Feed it into a fluid pump to lift the water onward.

Passthroughs & Outlets — masonry blocks with a pipe cast through them, so structure walls (boiler fireboxes, cowper stoves, furnaces) can be built across a pipe run. An outlet ends a run against a machine port face; topped with a vanilla chimney it instead vents the network's gas to the sky at ~16 L/s — the standard way to exhaust a firebox.

To build a minimal working steam line you need:

1. Water: place a Fluid Intake on a pond (full 3x3-deep water below it).
2. Boiler: build a Cornish Boiler with its firebox; fill it with water (buckets work for the first run) and light a coal pile behind its door. Vent the firebox outlet with a chimney.
3. Engine: build a Watt Engine nearby and pipe the boiler's steam outlet to the engine inlet through a Pressure Valve gated at ~2–3 atm (the Cornish boiler can out-pressure a Watt engine).
4. Sub-machine: drop a Fluid Pump or MP Generator into the engine's drive cell. Pump: pipe the fluid intake to its intake side and its output up into the boiler — the water loop closes itself. Generator: hook up axles and run your helve hammers and quern.
5. Pipe the engine's condensate drain back toward the boiler or a drain — and you have a self-feeding steam plant.

From here you can add more boilers and engines to produce mechanical power or transition to a Lancashire Boiler feeding a Cornish Engine. Best use for the steam power right now it to power the machines required for the blast furnace (air blowers) and the Bessemer converter from Steelmaking Expanded, so you can produce enough iron and steel to satisfy your Industrial Revolution needs.
